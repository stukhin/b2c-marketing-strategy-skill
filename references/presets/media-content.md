# Media / content / publishing

**Examples:** The Athletic, Substack writers at scale, Bloomberg consumer, The Information, Vox Media, BuzzFeed, niche national digital outlets, audio / podcast networks (Wondery, Pushkin), creator-led media platforms.

**Typical segments:** Heavy daily readers (often paid), occasional readers (ad-supported tier), category-specific deep readers (sports / finance / tech), email-only subscribers, social-referral one-time visitors, lapsed subscribers, B2B / institutional readers.

**Channel split benchmark:** Performance 25-35 / Brand 25-35 / Influence 5-12 / CRM 18-25 / Tools+Agencies 4-8 / Reserve 3

**KPI metrics:** Paid subscribers, free-to-paid conversion rate, churn (monthly + annual), engagement (active days per month, articles per visit), email open rate, time-on-platform, content-per-subscriber ratio, contribution margin per subscriber.

**Category risks:** Platform-dependence (Google / Meta traffic shifts), ad-blocker penetration eroding ad-supported tier, content-IP commoditization by AI summaries, talent / writer churn, content quality drift under cost-cutting, paywall fatigue.

**Strategically distinctive:** Two-sided product (creator economy applies — writers / journalists are part of the supply side). Subscriber LTV is the financial engine, not subscriber count. CRM and lifecycle work is unusually high-weight compared to other categories — re-engagement campaigns and winback flows drive material LTV. Brand work has dual purpose: consumer awareness AND creator recruitment ("places where great writers want to publish").

**Radar axes (Competitors section):** Content quality · Topic breadth vs depth · Voice / editorial point-of-view · Subscriber experience (UX, app, email) · Price-to-value · Trust / accuracy reputation

**Brand-funnel benchmark (category leader, Mode 1 hypothesis anchor):** TOM 8–20% (highly market-dependent; niche-leaders can hit 20%+ within their vertical despite low general-population awareness) · Aided 35–60% · Consideration 18–32% · Trial / 90-day-active 10–22%
