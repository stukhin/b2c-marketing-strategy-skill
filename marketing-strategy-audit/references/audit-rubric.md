# Audit rubric — scoring 0-100 per criterion

For each of the 7 criteria in `audit-criteria.md`, score the document on a 0–100 scale using the bands below. Composite score = unweighted average across the criteria that apply for the chosen mode: **avg of 6 in Quick score** (Reality check is skipped — 7 minus 1 leaves 6, not 5), **avg of 7 in Full audit**.

---

## Universal scoring bands

| Band | Range | Means |
|---|---|---|
| Excellent | 80–100 | Criterion fully addressed. Document would survive board / agency / investor scrutiny on this dimension. |
| Solid | 60–79 | Criterion mostly addressed. A few gaps to strengthen but no critical holes. |
| Mixed | 40–59 | Criterion partly addressed. Half the document does this well, half does it poorly. Visible improvement opportunity. |
| Weak | 20–39 | Criterion barely addressed. Strategy is exposed on this dimension. |
| Missing | 0–19 | Criterion not addressed. Document is functionally silent on this. |

---

## Per-criterion anchors

### Specificity

- **100** — Every major claim carries a specific number, every initiative carries a budget figure, every KPI has a target with a deadline
- **80** — All hero KPIs have targets; budget broken down by channel; most initiatives have figures
- **60** — KPIs have targets but budget is one lump sum; some initiatives have figures
- **40** — Top 2-3 KPI targets named, rest is vague; budget mentioned but not broken down
- **20** — A scattering of numbers but most claims are generic
- **0** — Reads as marketing copy. No concrete numbers anywhere

### Verification

- **100** — Every number traces to a named source. Internal data attributes tracker + date + sample size
- **80** — Public data sourced; internal data attributed; T1+T2 sources dominate
- **60** — Most public numbers sourced; some internal numbers float without attribution
- **40** — Occasional citations but most numbers are uncited
- **20** — Numbers present, sources absent
- **0** — No citations anywhere

### Risk register quality

- **100** — 5–8 risks with probability × impact × owner × mitigation × trigger. Anti-linearity satisfied (off-diagonal in both directions). Monthly cadence on critical risks
- **80** — 5+ risks with most structural elements; anti-linearity mostly satisfied
- **60** — Risks listed with probability+impact; mitigations vague; missing some structural elements
- **40** — Risks named but flat (all medium-medium); no off-diagonal
- **20** — 1-2 risks mentioned as afterthought
- **0** — No risk section

### Action specificity

- **100** — Every initiative: owner + deadline + success metric + dependencies
- **80** — Most initiatives have owner + deadline + success metric
- **60** — Initiatives have 2 of 3 (typically missing success metric or owner)
- **40** — Initiatives named but mostly missing operational detail
- **20** — A few initiatives have owners; most read as wishlist
- **0** — List of intentions with no execution structure

### Synthesis integrity

- **100** — Exec is a faithful precis of appendix. Every exec claim traces to appendix evidence. Metric vocabulary consistent throughout
- **80** — Exec mostly aligned; 1 minor disconnect
- **60** — Exec aligned on 2-3 of 5 hero claims; rest don't trace cleanly
- **40** — Exec and appendix feel like different documents
- **20** — Exec is marketing copy; appendix is unrelated analysis
- **0** — No coherent synthesis layer at all

### Reality check *(Full audit only)*

- **100** — Every major claim survives reality check. Brand reputation, market position, momentum all confirmed externally
- **80** — Most claims survive; 1 minor gap
- **60** — Half the claims confirmed, half contradicted by signals
- **40** — Multiple claims contradicted by observable signals
- **20** — Most claims contradicted
- **0** — Document is in a different reality from observable signals

### Completeness

- **100** — All 9 standard blocks present and substantive (market / consumer / competitive / positioning / KPI tree / media / budget / risk / next steps)
- **80** — 8 of 9 present; 1 thin
- **60** — 6-7 of 9 present
- **40** — 4-5 of 9 present
- **20** — 2-3 blocks present
- **0** — Under 2 blocks present — not a strategy, a fragment

---

## Composite score interpretation

| Composite | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 80–100 | **Boardroom-ready.** A few sharpening notes, otherwise ready for senior audience |
| 60–79 | **Director-ready.** Solid working draft with 3–5 specific gaps to address |
| 40–59 | **Working draft.** Substantial work remains. Half the dimensions need attention |
| 20–39 | **Early draft.** Document is a starting point, not a strategy. Most dimensions need work |
| 0–19 | **Not yet a strategy.** Document is a positioning statement or campaign brief mislabeled as strategy |

---

## Output presentation

Score each criterion 0–100. Present in the dashboard:

- **Big number** — composite score, large font
- **One-sentence headline** — name the dominant pattern. Examples:
  - "Synthesis-strong, risk-weak — exec is sharp, but the risk register isn't board-grade yet"
  - "Data-rich but specificity-poor — lots of numbers, few targets"
  - "Complete in structure, thin in evidence — all 9 sections present but most claims uncited"
  - "Tactic-heavy, strategy-light — lots of channel ideas, no positioning anchor"
- **Per-criterion bar chart** — 0-100 bars per criterion, color-coded by band
- **Top 2 strengths and top 2 weaknesses** below the chart, named explicitly

The score is the entry point. The gap list and recommendations sections do the actual heavy lifting.
