# Ride-hail & mobility

**Examples:** Uber, Lyft, Bolt, DiDi, Grab, Ola, Cabify

**Typical segments:** Daily commuters, occasional users (events / airport), business riders, drivers (full-time vs gig).

**Channel split benchmark:** Performance 35-45 / Brand 15-25 / Influence 5-10 / CRM 12-18 / Tools+Agencies 5-10 / Reserve 5

**KPI metrics:** Trips, MAU, take rate, driver supply hours, surge frequency, NPS, regulatory cost per trip.

**Category risks:** Driver supply scarcity, regulator caps / labor reclassification, competitor price wars, modal substitution (e-bikes / public transit).

**Strategically distinctive:** Two-sided: driver acquisition often higher CAC than rider. Geo-density / liquidity is the moat. Brand work skews trust + reliability.

**Radar axes (Competitors section):** Fare flexibility · Supply liquidity / wait time · Coverage · Trust & safety · Driver economics · Brand recall

**Brand-funnel benchmark (category leader, Mode 1 hypothesis anchor):** TOM 25–40% (Uber / Bolt-led) · Aided 70–85% · Consideration 40–55% · Trial / 90-day-active 30–45%
